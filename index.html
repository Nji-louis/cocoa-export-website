Our Export Process

Our export workflow quarantees consistent quality, full traceability, and seamless delivery to international ports.

  • 1. Farming & Harvesting: Cocoa is harvested from partner farmers across Cameroon's major cocoa regions.
  • 2. Fermentation: Beans ferment for 5-7 days developing thier rich flavor and aroma.
  • 3. Sun Drying: Beans are sun-dried to the optimal moisture level for export.
  • 4. Sorting & Grading: Defects are removed and only export-grade beans are selected.
  • 5. Bagging & Packaging: Beans are packed in clean jute bags with traceability labels.
  • 6. Warehouse Quality CHecks: Moisture testing, sampling, and fumigation when required.
  • 7. Export Documentation: phytosanitary-certificate, certificate of origin, export license & inspection reports.
  • 8. Transprt to Port: Logistics are arranged to the port of Douala or Kribi.
  • 9. Loading & Shipping: Containers are sealed and shipped to global destinations.
  • 10. Buyer Delivery: All documentation is provided, and buyers recieve tracking & support.

Cocoa Export & Customs Clearance

CHOCAM S.A.R.L Ltd ensures full compliance with Cameroon's cocoa export regulations and international trade standards.

Pre-Export Formalities

  • Register CHOCAM S.A.R.L Ltd in the Ministry of Trade exporters‚Äô file.
  • Obtain taxpayer card & customs registration.
  • Secure all permits and commercial documentation.
  • Obtain Certificate of Origin, Phytosanitary Certificate, and Stuffing Certificate.
  • Quality verification through accredited agencies (e.g., SGS).
üí∞

Taxation & Single Window

  • Submit documents to GUCE (Single Window for Foreign Trade).
  • Obtain Bordereau de Taxation (BDT) for exit duties.
  • Pay exit duty (typically 10% of FOB value).
  • Pay royalties required by the National Cocoa & Coffee Board (ONCC).
üìÇ

Export Declaration

  • Submit export declaration via SYDONIA++ system.
  • Customs verification of liquidation documents.
  • Provide all required certificates and quality verification documents.
‚úÖ

Good-to-Ship (GTS)

  • Customs grants GTS authorization after checks.
  • Physical inspection may be conducted before cargo leaves storage.
  • Present waybill/export manifest at exit points (port/land/river).
üö¢

Loading & Export

  • Load cocoa into containers using stuffing certificate.
  • Carrier issues Bill of Lading and transport documentation.
  • Final clearance by customs after GTS approval.
üì¶

Post-Export Compliance

  • Retain all export documentation for audits.
  • Traceability for zero-deforestation certified cocoa.
  • Report export volumes & quality to ONCC.
